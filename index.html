Profile

I grew up in Gippsland, Victoria, and have lived in small country towns my whole life. I started my working life on remote Western Australian cattle stations, an environment where my natural ingenuity and determination flourished. During this time, I developed a passion for building and fabrication, which led me to completing a boiler making trade, predominantly in the offshore Oil and Gas sector. I then successful pursued supervision and inspection qualifications related to my trade. My current career involves asset integrity inspection, and systems development for an engineering and NDT service provider.

I have always been intrigued by the intricacies of how things work, occasionally to excess. As a kid I was more likely to disassemble the lawnmower than mow the lawns!

When not working I enjoy time with my wife and two children, and our two cats and dogs. Some of my hobbies include hunting, motorbike riding, metal, wood, and electronics projects.

Why IT?

I have always enjoyed creating things, which has led me to a passion for understanding the mechanics and intricacies of things. Prior to 2018 my exposure to computers and IT was very limited due to my occupation. In 2018 I began a new chapter in my career, taking on the position of operations support technician with a new company. The company had no local IT support, and as part of the role I was working with large volumes of data using a specialist piece of software. I developed a reputation for being “good with computers” amongst my team and was assigned responsibilities for managing and maintaining all the IT infrastructure, including end user support. I was also able to explore solutions to several functional deficiencies in the software and systems being utilized. Since then I have developed a SharePoint site with extensive automation which has become the model for similar projects around the world. I have also worked with software suppliers to make improvements to their products and am currently working to integrate these improvements into the company’s wider digital environment.

This exposure triggered my innate desire to grow and develop new skills, with IT providing a vast array of opportunities to improve the world around me.

I chose to study at RMIT due to the reputation for delivering exceptional course content coupled with the flexible delivery method, which positions this university as a leader in the sector. I intend to further my education and pursue a Bachelor’s degree. Within this courses content there is detailed, but wide-ranging coverage of the field. The 100% online delivery is also essential to my situation as attending on premises is not possible given my location, family, and work commitments.

Upon completion of the bachelor’s degree I am confident I will have a solid general IT skillset, and have gained fundamental knowledge in a broad range of IT specialities. While I come to this course with some self-learned skills, a structured and proven curriculum will bridge gaps in my experience. Exposure to ideas, concepts, and the tertiary thought process will be valuable skills as I continue to develop as an IT professional.

My ideal job

Data engineer - CSIRO Data61

This position is a data engineer working for the CSIRO (Commonwealth Scientific and Industrial Research Organisation), the Australian national science agency. The position advertised is responsible for providing access to information and predictions extracted from the available data. End users include researchers, industry, and general public, delivered through a variety of pathways. The ability to work with the country’s leading researchers, developing new technologies and insights fits with my passions, and is the primary reason for pursuing this position. As a dedicated research institution, the CSIRO presents an opportunity to be involved with a range of important and exiting projects.

This position requires the skillset and abilities to extract information and predictions from data and deliver to cloud platforms. There is a strong emphasis on capabilities with machine learning, cloud computing and software development as well as generalised critical thinking skills. As this role is part of a large and highly diverse team, the ability to operate effectively in this environment is also essential. The position description specifies a relevant tertiary qualification or equivalent experience in software engineering is required to be considered as an applicant.

Currently I have experience contributing to highly technical projects across a wide variety of disciplines, often with competing priorities. My strong critical thinking abilities have allowed me to provide valuable insight into the projects I have been involved with I have personally developed and managed operational environments, and possess some previous knowledge and exposure to cloud platforms and the needs of managing and maintaining them effectively. My software development experience is currently limited to small, unmanaged, limited exposure projects.

To develop the capabilities required to successfully pursue this role, I will complete a Bachelor’s degree in IT with RMIT, with a focus on programming and cloud platforms. Post graduate studies in computer science specialities, predominately machine learning and Artificial Intelligence will further contribute to the skillset required. Once I have gained the required qualifications, knowledge and skillset, I will the seek roles that provide sufficient opportunity to implement these skills. Further to gaining the appropriate practical experience, I will be a leading candidate for this and similar positions.
